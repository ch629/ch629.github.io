# OpenCode Project Context

## What this project is
- Personal website + blog for Charlie Howes (senior backend contractor).
- Stack is static-site focused (Hugo now; keep content structure friendly for Jekyll-style front matter where practical).
- Primary business goal is qualified inbound leads for contract backend work.

## Positioning
- Core niche: fintech backend delivery (Go, payments systems, distributed/event-driven architecture, migrations, reliability).
- Broader fit: open to any backend system, with primary implementation focus in Go.
- Target buyers: CTOs, engineering managers, and fintech/platform teams.
- Brand tone: credible, technical, direct, low-hype.

## What "good" looks like
- Homepage explains who I help + outcomes in under 10 seconds.
- Services and experience pages show concrete delivery outcomes and trust signals.
- Every key page has one clear primary CTA.
- Content is useful to technical buyers and supports hiring decisions.

## Content and UX guardrails
- Prefer specific, outcome-led copy over generic claims.
- Keep pages concise and scannable (clear headings, short sections, concrete bullets).
- Keep design changes consistent with existing site style unless asked to redesign.
- Always preserve or improve mobile readability and load performance.

## SEO and discoverability guardrails
- Ensure strong `title` and `description` front matter for key pages/posts.
- Use internal links from blog posts to services/contact pages when relevant.
- Prefer buyer-intent topics (case studies, migration playbooks, architecture tradeoffs) over broad tutorials.

## Conversion guardrails
- Primary CTA default: email for project discussion.
- Secondary CTA default: LinkedIn message.
- Reduce contact friction (clear contact path, minimal steps, visible CTA placement).
- Highlight proof where possible: outcomes, roles, systems scale, reliability wins.

## Working preferences for OpenCode sessions
- Prioritize high-impact 80/20 improvements first.
- Give implementable recommendations, not generic options.
- When suggesting changes, include: why it matters + expected impact.
- Keep outputs concise unless I ask for deep detail.
- Writing style preference: technical-first, while surfacing business outcomes where they are clear and relevant.

## Build and cleanup workflow
- After making site/content/template changes, always run `hugo build` to validate the site builds successfully.
- If `hugo build` fails, fix the issues and rerun until the build passes.
- After a successful build, remove generated files in `public/` to keep session changes clean.

## Copy style do/don't
- Do: use specific language, concrete examples, and measurable outcomes where available.
- Do: connect technical decisions to delivery speed, risk reduction, reliability, or business value.
- Do: keep claims evidence-backed and realistic.
- Don't: use hype, buzzword-heavy phrasing, or vague "expert" statements without proof.
- Don't: add fluff, filler intros, or generic advice that could apply to any contractor.

## If context is missing, assume
- Audience is technical and time-constrained.
- Goal is quality leads, not traffic volume.
- Preference is practical changes that can ship quickly.
- If assumptions appear weak or likely wrong, ask concise clarifying questions before proceeding.
